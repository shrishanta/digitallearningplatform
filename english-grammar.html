✏ English Basic Grammar Notes

1. Parts of Speech (Building blocks of sentences)

  1. Noun – Name of a person, place, thing, or idea.
    👉 Ram, school, book, love
  2. Pronoun – Words used instead of nouns.
    👉 he, she, it, they, we
  3. Verb – Action or state of being.
    👉 run, eat, is, are
  4. Adjective – Words that describe nouns.
    👉 tall boy, red car, sweet mango
  5. Adverb – Words that describe verbs/adjectives.
    👉 He runs quickly. She sings beautifully.
  6. Preposition – Shows relationship between words.
    👉 in, on, under, near, between
  7. Conjunction – Joins words or sentences.
    👉 and, but, or, because
  8. Interjection – Words showing sudden feelings.
    👉 Wow! Oh! Alas!

2. Sentence Structure

Subject – Who/what the sentence is about.

Predicate – What the subject does.

👉 Example: The cat (subject) is sleeping on the mat (predicate).

3. Types of Sentences

  1. Declarative – Statement → I am a student.
  2. Interrogative – Question → Are you a student?
  3. Imperative – Command/Request → Please sit down.
  4. Exclamatory – Strong feeling → What a beautiful day!

4. Tenses (Time of action)

  1. Present – I eat an apple.
  2. Past – I ate an apple.
  3. Future – I will eat an apple.

5. Articles

a, an → used with singular nouns.
👉 a book, an apple

the → used for specific things.
👉 the sun, the Taj Mahal

6. Punctuation

. (Full stop) – End of a sentence.

, (Comma) – Pause or separate items.

? (Question mark) – For questions.

! (Exclamation mark) – Strong feelings.

7. Common Grammar Rules

  • ✔ A sentence always starts with a capital letter.
  • ✔ Use is/are for present, was/were for past.
  • ✔ Singular noun → verb with s/es (He plays).
  • ✔ Plural noun → verb without s/es (They play).
