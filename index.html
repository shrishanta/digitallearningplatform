Maths Basics for Rural Students

1. Numbers & Counting

Learn numbers 1 to 100.

Practice counting using:

  • Seeds, grains, or stones.
  • Animals (cows, goats, chickens).
  • Household objects (pots, spoons, etc.).

Example:
If you have 5 cows and your neighbour has 3,
Total cows = 5 + 3 = 8.

2. Addition (+) and Subtraction (‚àí)

Use simple real-life examples:

  • Money transactions (buying vegetables, selling milk).

Example:

‚Çπ5 √ó 4 = ‚Çπ20.

4. Division (√∑)

Use sharing examples.

Example:
12 sweets shared among 4 kids:
12 √∑ 4 = 3 sweets each.

5. Shapes (Geometry Basics)

Identify shapes around them:

  • Circle: a plate, sun.
  • Square: tiles, fields.
  • Rectangle: door, books.
  • Triangle: roof.

6. Measurement Basics

Length: Use hand spans, footsteps, measuring tapes.

Weight: Use traditional scales, kilos.

Volume: Litres (milk, water).

7. Money & Simple Math

Recognize coins and notes.

Add/subtract money.

Learn to calculate simple totals.

8. Time & Calendar

Learn reading the clock.

Days, weeks, months, seasons.

Sunrise/sunset timing for daily planning.

9. Practical Fractions

Introduce halves and quarters using roti or chapati.

Example:
Cut 1 roti into 2 pieces = ¬Ω each.
Cut into 4 pieces = ¬º each.

10. Daily Life Math

Count seeds while planting.

Calculate cost of groceries.

Understand bus ticket fares and distance.

Physics Basics

Physics is the study of matter, energy, and their interactions. It explains how things move, why they move, and how forces work in nature.

Main Branches of Physics

  1. Mechanics ‚Äì Study of motion, forces, and energy.
  2. Optics ‚Äì Study of light and vision.
  3. Electricity & Magnetism ‚Äì Study of electric charges, current, and magnets.
  4. Thermodynamics ‚Äì Study of heat and temperature.
  5. Sound (Acoustics) ‚Äì Study of sound waves.
  6. Modern Physics ‚Äì Study of atoms, particles, and the universe.

Important Concepts

Concept Explanation Example
Matter Anything that has mass and takes up space. Water, air, rocks.
Force A push or pull on an object. Pushing a ball.
Motion Change of position of an object. A car moving on the road.
Speed How fast something moves. Car speed = distance/time.
Gravity A force that pulls objects toward Earth. Apple falling from a tree.
Work Done when a force moves an object. Lifting a box.
Energy Ability to do work. Heat, light, sound energy.
Friction Force that slows things down. Rubbing hands together.

Simple Physics Formulas

  1. Speed = Distance √∑ Time
  2. Force = Mass √ó Acceleration (Newton‚Äôs 2nd Law)
  3. Work = Force √ó Distance
  4. Power = Work √∑ Time
  5. Density = Mass √∑ Volume

Fun Examples in Real Life

  • A seesaw shows balance of forces.
  • A rainbow shows light refraction.
  • A fan works due to electric energy changing to motion.
  • A boat floats due to buoyancy.

Chemistry Basics

Chemistry is the science of matter ‚Äì it studies what things are made of, how they change, and how they interact with each other. It helps us understand everything around us: air, water, food, medicines, clothes, and even our own body!

Main Branches of Chemistry

  1. Organic Chemistry ‚Äì Study of carbon compounds (e.g., fuels, medicines).
  2. Inorganic Chemistry ‚Äì Study of non-carbon substances (e.g., metals, minerals).
  3. Physical Chemistry ‚Äì How energy and matter interact.
  4. Analytical Chemistry ‚Äì How to test and identify substances.
  5. Biochemistry ‚Äì Chemistry of living things.

Important Chemistry Concepts

Concept Simple Meaning Example
Matter Anything that has mass and takes space. Air, water, rock.
Atom Smallest unit of matter. Hydrogen atom.
Element A pure substance with only one kind of atom. Gold (Au), Oxygen (O).
Molecule Two or more atoms joined together. H‚ÇÇO (water).
Compound A substance made of different elements. Salt (NaCl).
Mixture Combination of substances not chemically joined. Air, salad.
Chemical Reaction When substances change to form new ones. Burning wood ‚Üí ash, smoke.

States of Matter

  1. Solid ‚Äì Fixed shape and volume (ice, rock).
  2. Liquid ‚Äì Fixed volume but takes container‚Äôs shape (water, oil).
  3. Gas ‚Äì No fixed shape or volume (air, steam).
  4. Plasma ‚Äì Super-hot gas (sun, stars).

Simple Chemistry Facts

  • Water is made of 2 hydrogen atoms + 1 oxygen atom (H‚ÇÇO).
  • Air is a mixture of gases (mostly nitrogen and oxygen).
  • Rust forms when iron reacts with oxygen and water.
  • Cooking is a chemical change.

Safety Rules in Chemistry

  • Always wear gloves, goggles, and apron.
  • Never taste or smell unknown chemicals.
  • Handle acids and bases carefully.
  • Wash hands after experiments.

Biology Basics for School Students

Biology is the study of living things ‚Äì plants, animals, humans, and microorganisms. It explains how they live, grow, reproduce, and interact with the environment.

Main Branches of Biology

  1. Botany ‚Äì Study of plants
  2. Zoology ‚Äì Study of animals
  3. Microbiology ‚Äì Study of tiny organisms (bacteria, fungi, viruses)
  4. Human Biology ‚Äì Study of the human body

Characteristics of Living Things (MRS GREN)

  • Movement ‚Äì move in some way
  • Respiration ‚Äì breathe/produce energy
  • Sensitivity ‚Äì respond to surroundings
  • Growth ‚Äì get bigger/mature
  • Reproduction ‚Äì make new life
  • Excretion ‚Äì remove waste
  • Nutrition ‚Äì need food for energy

The Cell ‚Äì Basic Unit of Life

All living things are made of cells. Cells are like tiny building blocks.

Types:

  • Plant cells (have cell wall and chloroplast for photosynthesis)
  • Animal cells (no cell wall, no chloroplast)

Plants vs Animals

Plants: Make their own food by photosynthesis (using sunlight, water, carbon dioxide).

Animals: Cannot make their own food; depend on plants or other animals.

Human Body Basics

  • Respiratory system ‚Äì helps us breathe.
  • Digestive system ‚Äì breaks food into energy.
  • Circulatory system ‚Äì heart pumps blood.
  • Nervous system ‚Äì brain controls actions.
  • Skeletal system ‚Äì bones give shape and support.

Why is Biology Important?

Helps us understand our body and health.

Helps farmers grow better crops.

Helps doctors find cures for diseases.

Explains nature, animals, and the environment.

In short: Biology = Study of Life
